Interesting content about the Camels:

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  4. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.

Interesting content about the CHAMELEONS:

  • ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR.
  • CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE.
  • THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
  • UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN'T REGROW THEIR TAILS.

Interesting content about the Cicadas:

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  3. They're true bugs (from the order Hemiptera).
  4. Most have red-orange eyes.

Interesting content about the Pandas:

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • Cubs are well protected in their first month.
  • They spend a lot of their day eating.

Interesting content about the Pigeons:

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  4. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Interesting content about the Teddy:

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
